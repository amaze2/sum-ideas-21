Digital Scholarly Edition Evaluation / Planning

Assignment

Choose a digital edition below, and examine / evaluate / reflect on it. Answer the questions below.
Your goal is get a sense of what others have done and what can be done to learn what a digital scholarly edition is and to decide what to do in your projects.

  • What do you like / dislike about it? Why?
  • What scholarly apparatus elements would you like to emulate / not emulate in your digital edition?
    • Annotations: is the text(s) annotated? What is annotated? How much of the text is marked up? How much info is provided? Are the annotations in note or glossary format?
    • Style: is the text layed out or styled in a helpful way?
    • Context: How is the text(s) contextualized? What contextual materials are provided? Are they included in / alongside the text (links, embedded images, etc.)? Or are they excluded to a separate section? Is their relationship to the text explained, or are they left to speak for themselves?
    • Interpretation: How is the text interpreted? Are there any computational text analyses + visualizations? Are they explained?
    • Biography / Chronology: how does it illuminate the author's life?
    • Introduction: How is the author introduced?
  • What does the digital format enable that can't be done in print?
    (and what does the digital disable that can be done in print?)
  • How is the reading experience different / better / worse than a print edition?

Additional Questions to Consider:

  • Creator(s): Who created / is in charge of the digital edition?
  • Platform: How is it hosted / published? A digital publishing platform (which?)? Or some other way?
  • Access: Is it for web reading only, or can it also be printed?
  • Cost: Is it free / do you have to pay to use it?
  • Interactivity: Does it solicit user interaction or user-generated content?
  • Documentation: Is the site well explained?
  • Audience: Who is its audience(s)?
  • Responsive: Is it just as usable on a cell phone as on a PC?
  • Longevity: How long has it existed? Is it being maintained / updated? Or is it abandoned?
